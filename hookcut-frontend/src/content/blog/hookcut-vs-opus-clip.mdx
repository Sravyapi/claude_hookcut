If you've been looking for an AI tool to turn your YouTube videos into Shorts, OpusClip is probably the first name you encountered. It's well-funded, well-marketed, and widely used. But after a few weeks of using it, many creators hit the same wall: **too many clips, not enough quality**.

## What OpusClip actually does

OpusClip is an AI video clipper. You upload a video (or paste a YouTube URL), and it generates multiple clips — typically 10 to 15 — scored and ready to post. It's fast, polished, and has strong social sharing features.

The core model is built around **volume**: give the creator as many clips as possible and let them decide. It works well if you want to move fast and are comfortable sorting through mediocre output to find the gems.

**OpusClip strengths:**
- Large clip count per analysis
- Social media scheduling integration
- Clean, polished UI
- Strong branding features (logo placement, subtitles)

**OpusClip limitations:**
- No explanation of *why* a clip was chosen
- Clip quality is inconsistent — filler content and mid-sentence cuts are common
- India pricing is high (USD-only billing)
- No concept of "hook type" or engagement classification

## What HookCut actually does

HookCut is not a video clipper — it's a **hook analysis engine**. The difference is intentional.

Rather than generating 10–15 clips and making you sort through them, HookCut analyzes your video's transcript, identifies the 5 moments with the highest scroll-stopping potential, scores each one, and classifies them by hook type. You then select up to 3 to turn into Shorts.

This means you get fewer clips — but every clip was chosen because it has a reason to stop a scroll.

**HookCut strengths:**
- Hook Score (0–10) on every moment
- 18 hook type classifications (curiosity gap, shock statistic, open loop, etc.)
- Explanation of why each hook works (Platform Dynamics, Viewer Psychology, Creator Tip)
- Penalty filter — filler intros, sponsor segments, and mid-sentence cuts are automatically rejected
- India pricing (₹499/month, UPI accepted)

**HookCut limitations:**
- Maximum 5 hooks per analysis (quality over quantity)
- No built-in scheduling (download and post manually)
- Newer product, smaller user base

## The key difference: why each clip was chosen

This is where the tools diverge most clearly.

In OpusClip, you see a clip and a score. The score is a general "virality" estimate. There's no breakdown, no hook type, no explanation of what makes this specific moment compelling.

In HookCut, every hook comes with:
- A score from 0–10
- A hook type classification
- A note on platform dynamics ("This works on Shorts because...")
- A viewer psychology note ("Audiences stop here because...")
- A creator tip ("Consider starting 4 seconds earlier to capture the setup")

For creators who want to learn — not just clip — this context is the product.

## Clip quality: what actually gets through

Both tools use AI to identify clip-worthy moments. But they differ in what they *reject*.

OpusClip surfaces everything above a minimum threshold. That means you'll regularly see:

- Clips that start with "Hey guys, welcome back..."
- Clips that cut mid-sentence
- Sponsor segment clips
- Clips from Q&A intros with no hook

HookCut applies a quality filter before you see anything. Moments that don't meet engagement thresholds are removed automatically. You only see moments with a genuine reason to stop a scroll.
