When Ravi launched his YouTube channel in 2023, he had a clear formula: long-form personal finance education, detailed breakdowns, and honest takes on Indian investing. By late 2024, he had built a loyal audience of 45,000 subscribers — but his Shorts weren't connecting.

"I was taking clips from my long videos and posting them as Shorts," he says. "They'd get 2,000–3,000 views if I was lucky. My long videos were doing better than my Shorts, which felt backwards."

The problem wasn't the content. It was the clips.

---

## The Video

In November 2024, Ravi published a 45-minute breakdown of mutual fund fees — specifically, the hidden expense ratios that most investors don't know they're paying. The video performed well by his standards: 82,000 views, strong watch time, and dozens of comments from viewers who said they immediately checked their portfolios.

"I knew there was gold in that video," Ravi says. "But every time I tried to clip it, I'd end up with something like 'Today I want to talk about mutual funds' — which is technically accurate but does nothing."

He tried clipping manually, starting from the parts of the video he thought were most important. None of the clips crossed 5,000 views.

---

## What HookCut Found

Ravi ran the video through HookCut and reviewed the five hook moments it surfaced.

The highest-scoring clip didn't start where he expected. It began at 18:34 — roughly 18 minutes into the video — at a moment he described as a "throwaway line" when he was recording.

The clip opened with: *"Your mutual fund is quietly taking ₹40,000 from you every year. Not in fees you can see. In returns you never get."*

"I didn't think of that as a hook when I said it," Ravi says. "I was setting up a calculation. But HookCut scored it extremely high — I think it was 91 — and I understood why immediately when I saw it in isolation. That line, out of context, is actually a perfect hook."

The clip ran for 47 seconds. Ravi made no edits, added captions with the Clean style, and posted it.

---

## The Results

The Short reached 2.1 million views over the following 6 weeks.

At its peak, it was driving 800–1,000 new subscribers per day to Ravi's channel. His long-form video about mutual fund fees — the source of the clip — saw its view count jump from 82,000 to 340,000 as Short viewers converted to watch the full breakdown.

"The viral Short became the trailer for the long video," he says. "And the long video was where the real audience building happened."

---

## The Hook That Made It Work

The opening line worked because it created immediate financial stakes. Every person watching who owns a mutual fund — which in India is a very large and growing audience — has a direct personal reason to keep watching.

It also used a specific number (₹40,000) rather than a vague claim ("a lot"). Specificity is one of the strongest markers of credibility in financial content. And it framed the loss as something invisible — "not in fees you can see" — which creates both tension and a curiosity gap that the rest of the clip resolves.

Ravi didn't write that opening as a hook. He said it because it was true, and he was in the middle of making a point. The hook was already in the video. HookCut found it.

## What Changed After

Since that Short, Ravi has run 14 more videos through HookCut. His Shorts channel average has moved from 2,800 views per Short to 47,000.

"The thing I didn't expect," he says, "is that running my videos through HookCut also made me a better creator. I started to notice, when I was recording, which moments had hook potential. I'd think about the opening line differently. It's changed how I write scripts."

His channel crossed 128,000 subscribers in February 2026.
